Description:


Cardiology ,(cardio means heart and logy means study) is a branch of medicine dealing with disorders of the heart as well as parts of the circulatory system. A Cardiologist is a physician who specialises in diagnosing , treating and preventing diseases of te cardiovascular system , which includes the heart and the blood vessels. They are highly specialized. Some of the diagnostic tools used by cardiologists include echocardiograms , cardiac catheterizations and exercise tests. Patients are typically referred to cardiologists by their regular doctors. These patients may be experiencing symptoms that indicate heart trouble. Cardiologists diagnose and treat heart and blood vessel problems using a combination of physical examination, symptom review and medical testing. Some cardiologists work in private practice centers as consultants to patients' general care practitioners, while others work for universities as researchers and teachers. As part of their practice, cardiologists may perform or recommend procedures like echocardiograms, exercise tests and cardiac catheterizations.

Job Description :


Cardiologists are responsible for caring patients with diseases or deformities in heart or vascular system. There are mainly two parts in cardiologist job description, cardiologist and interventional cardiologist. The former specializes in diagnosing, preventing and treating diseases in cardiovascular system that includes heart and blood vessels. These professionals also diagnose and treat injury in heart and vascular system. Interventional cardiologists, on the other hand, perform a number of invasive tests and procedures to assist in treatments and diagnoses of cardiac disease. A cardio-vascular or cardiac surgeon can be a cardiologist who specializes in the surgery of heart and vascular system. Cardiologists use diagnostic tools such as echocardiograms, exercise tests, cardiac catheterizations. Cardiologists diagnose and treat problems in heart and blood vessels by using a combination of physical examination, medical testing and symptom review.

How To Become Cardiologist:


If you want to become a cardiologist, you must be aware that many years of education and training are required to reach this position. High school education is the first position. Those who are interested in medical career need to take classes in sciences, which include biology, physiology, anatomy, advanced math, physics and chemistry. Taking four year bachelor degree from an accredited university or college is the next step. The course should be in a life science. Pre-med students must take courses in chemistry, biology, math, physics and English. You can gain practical experience by doing intern clinics or hospitals. Competition in medical school entrance is extremely fierce. So, if you want to get an admission in a medical school, you must have high grades, top scores on MCAT exam and successful interviews. Once enter in a medical school, you are required to undergo 4-years of intense academic curriculum, which consists of classwork and clinical experience. During the first two years, you will learn general science studies, which include anatomy, microbiology, embryology, pathology, pharmacology, and physics. Part 1 of USMLE exam should be taken after completing first 2 years. If you pass the exam, you can continue the remaining 2 years that focus on clinical training. Graduates who successfully complete the medical school must enroll in a 3-years residency program in internal medicine to become a cardiologist. Resident doctors can work under the close supervision of licensed doctors. However, the tasks and functions of both will be same. Before becoming a licensed cardiologist, you must also obtain a 2-3 years fellowship in cardiology. If you want to specialize in cardiology, you can select: * Cardiac electrophysiology * Interventional cardiology * Echocardiography * Nuclear cardiology If you want to become a specialist, extra years of education and training is needed. You must earn a license to work as a cardiologist in every state in the US. Certification for cardiologists is provided by the American Board of Medical Specialties. It is also essential to get certificates in two specialties, cardiology and internal medicine.
